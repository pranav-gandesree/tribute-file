Virat Kohli

The man who changed the way of cricket

Here's a timeline of Virat's life:

  • 1988 - Born in New Delhi, India

  • 1998 - Started his cricket career at the academy under Rajkumar Sharma

  • 2002 - Kohli first played for Delhi Under-15 team in the 2002-03 Polly Umrigar Trophy.

  • 2004 - In late 2004, he was selected in the Delhi Under-17 team for the 2003-04 Vijay Merchant Trophy.

  • 2006 - In July 2006, Kohli was selected in the India Under-19 squad on its tour of England.

  • 2006 - Kohli's father died on 18 December 2006 due to a stroke after being bed-ridden for a month.

  • 2008 - In February-March 2008, Kohli captained the victorious Indian team at the 2008 ICC Under-19 Cricket World Cup held in Malaysia.

  • 2008 - Following the Under-19 World Cup, Kohli was bought by the Indian Premier League franchise Royal Challengers Bangalore for $30,000 on a youth contract.

  • 2008 - He made his international debut, at the age of 19.In August 2008, Kohli was included in the Indian ODI squad for tour of Sri Lanka and the Champions Trophy in Pakistan.

  • 2011 - Kohli played in every match of India's successful World Cup campaign.

    In the final against Sri Lanka at Mumbai, he scored 35, sharing an 83-run partnership with Gambhir. India went on to win the match by six wickets and lift the World Cup for the first time since 1983.


  • 2012 - Kohli was appointed the vice-captain for the 2012 Asia Cup in Bangladesh on the back of his fine performance in Australia.

  • 2013 - In March 2013, Kohli started a charity foundation called Virat Kohli Foundation (VKF). The organisation aims at helping underprivileged kids and conducts events to raise funds for the charity.

    In June 2013, Kohli featured in the ICC Champions Trophy in England which India won.


  • 2016 - At the 2016 IPL, the Royal Challengers finished runners-up and Kohli broke the record for most runs in an IPL season (of 733 runs) by scoring 973 runs in 16 matches winning the Orange Cap as well as Most-valuable Player Award of Vivo IPL 2016.

  • 2016 - Indian Player Virat Kohli became the first batsman in the history of Test cricket to score four double hundreds in four consecutive Test series.

  • 2017 - Indian cricket captain Virat Kohli was awarded the Padma Shri award by President Pranab Mukherjee at a ceremony at the Rashtrapati Bhavan.

  • 2017 - In 2017, Kohli was ranked 7th in the list released by Forbes as the Most Valuable Brand among athlete with an estimated brand value of $14.5 million, Making him the only Indian and the only cricketer to feature in that list.

  • 2018 - Men's cricket team captain Virat Kohli was provided with the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna Award by President Ram Nath Kovind at the Rashtrapati Bhavan.
